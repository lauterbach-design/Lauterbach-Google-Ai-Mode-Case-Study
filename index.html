01 · The Challenge

Search is changing.
The UX hasn't caught up.

🧠 Cognitive Overload

Traditional search presents 10 blue links. Users must mentally synthesize multiple pages to answer multi-part or complex questions — a process requiring 3–6 separate searches on average for research tasks.

🔍 Zero-Click Fragmentation

As AI summaries appear at the top of results, the user journey fragments. There's no coherent conversational layer — follow-up questions restart from scratch, losing context and continuity.

📱 Mobile Experience Gap

AI-generated answers are desktop-first. On mobile — where 63% of Google searches happen — the experience collapses into truncated, unactionable summaries that fail to support nuanced queries.

🔗 Trust & Citation Deficit

Early AI search features surfaced answers without transparent sourcing. Users couldn't verify information, leading to low confidence scores (38% trust) for AI-generated content in user testing.

"People don't want 10 answers to sort through. They want one good answer they can trust — and the ability to go deeper when they need to."
— Synthesis from 24 user interviews, Google Search UX research 2024
02 · The Solution

A conversational AI assistant
that thinks with you.

🌊

Streaming AI Responses

Answers appear in real-time via token streaming — giving users immediate feedback and reducing perceived wait time by 60%. The progressive reveal mimics natural reading, building trust as content arrives.

💬

Conversational Follow-ups

Users can refine, challenge, or extend any AI response without losing context. The assistant maintains full conversation history — enabling multi-turn research sessions that build toward complex conclusions.

📎

Inline Source Citations

Every factual claim links to its source inline — not buried in a footnotes list. Users hover or tap to preview sources without leaving the conversation, resolving the trust deficit identified in research.

🔍

Deep Search Integration

When AI Mode needs current data, it performs live web searches and surfaces the results within the conversational thread — combining the freshness of search with the synthesis of AI.

🗂️

Structured Answer Formats

Complex answers use adaptive formatting — timelines for historical queries, comparison tables for product research, step-by-step guides for how-to questions — matching format to intent automatically.

✏️

Editable Query Interface

Users can edit any past message in the thread to branch the conversation, run alternative scenarios, or correct a misunderstood premise — a non-linear exploration model new to search UX.

05 · Key Learnings

What I'd tell my
past self about AI UX.

  • 01

    Streaming is a UX feature, not just an implementation detail

    The animation speed of streaming text dramatically affects perceived intelligence and trustworthiness. Too fast feels synthetic; too slow creates anxiety. Finding the right cadence (roughly 25–40 tokens/sec rendered) was one of the most impactful micro-decisions in the project.

  • 02

    Vibe coding doesn't replace design thinking — it accelerates it

    AI Studio's code generation is powerful, but directionless without strong UX intent. The quality of the prototype output was proportional to the quality of the design decisions made before opening the tool. Garbage in, garbage out still applies.

  • 03

    Citation design is its own discipline

    Where, when, and how citations appear shapes user trust more than answer quality alone. Inline citations outperformed footnote-style attribution by 34% in trust scores — but required careful typographic treatment to avoid cluttering the reading experience.

  • 04

    Design for uncertainty — AI isn't always confident

    The most important edge case is when the model is uncertain or wrong. Designing clear confidence indicators, graceful "I don't know" states, and easy correction pathways was as important as designing for the success state.
